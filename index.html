Welcome Note

Leadership involves understanding how to inspire, influence and control how people behave.

Emotional intelligence therefore provides leaders with the capacity for Interpersonal Intelligence, which as to do with the ability to observe and understand your team, what motivates them, how they work and how to work cooperatively with them.

Emotional Intelligence helps leaders to be an active listener. Good listeners are genuinely interested, empathetic and concerned to find out what is going on and create an environment where problems can be discussed so that solution can be found.

To conclude with, as a leader you necessarily need not to shout, or boom your voice or have physical stature before you can communicate and put your team on track. All that matters is to develop the necessary skill that makes an ideal leader.

EMOTIONAL MANAGEMENT.

The first step in regulating and managing emotions is learning to identify and label them as they occur. This ability often requires not only the ability to observe one’s emotional responses in situations but also to describe with a sense of accuracy the context in which the emotions occur.

Emotional management been the ability to realize, readily accept, as well as successfully control feelings in oneself and having a complete authority over changes in ones thoughts and feelings that are generated whenever ones values are touched by the actions of a person or an event. Emotions can be positive or negative, giving pleasure or discomfort. The feeling can be slight or intense. It can also last for a shorter or longer period.
